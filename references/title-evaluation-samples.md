## Observations

- The formula strongly prefers clean product identification and true differentiators.
- Brand-sensitive categories such as tumblers benefit from the `B` variable.
- `T` and `V` help suppress noisy marketplace titles that mix target groups or enumerate too many options.
- Some market titles still score well when they are short, clear, and commercially natural even if they are slightly less descriptive.

## Additional Observations

- Footwear and bedding confirm that `B` should keep meaningful weight because brand, line, and model cues often matter.
- Functional everyday goods such as shower heads produce many near-ties, which suggests the engine is appropriately neutral when titles are all short and clear.
- `V` is especially useful in luggage and marketplace-heavy apparel or sock titles where sellers enumerate too many variants.
- `S` helps separate acceptable but awkward titles from clean commerce-style titles without overpowering the core relevance signal.
